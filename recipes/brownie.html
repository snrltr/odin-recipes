Odin Brownie Recipe

Recipe Description

Rich, fudgy, and irresistibly chocolatey, these classic brownies are everything a brownie should be. With a crackly top and a dense, gooey center, they strike the perfect balance between chewy and soft. Whether you enjoy them plain, studded with chocolate chunks, or served warm with a scoop of ice cream, this timeless recipe is guaranteed to satisfy any chocolate craving.

Ingredients:

  • 170g (¾ cup) unsalted butter
  • 200g (1 cup) granulated sugar
  • 150g (¾ cup packed) brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs + 1 egg yolk
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 90g (¾ cup) unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 100g (¾ cup) all-purpose flour
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 120g (¾ cup) dark chocolate chips or chunks (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 175°C (350°F): Grease or line an 8-inch (20cm) square baking pan with parchment paper, leaving a slight overhang for easy removal.
  2. Melt the butter: In a medium saucepan over low heat, melt the butter. Remove from heat and whisk in both sugars until combined and glossy.
  3. Add wet ingredients: Whisk in the eggs and yolk, one at a time, followed by the vanilla extract.
  4. Add dry ingredients: Sift in the cocoa powder, flour, and salt. Stir gently until just combined - don't overmix. Fold in chocolate chips or chunks if using.
  5. Bake: Pour the batter into the prepared pan and smooth the top. Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted comes out with a few moist crumbs (not wet batter).
  6. Cool & serve: Let brownies cool in the pan for 10-15 minutes, then lift out using the parchment overhang. Cool completely before slicing into squares for clean edges and maximum fudginess.
