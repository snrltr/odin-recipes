Odin Chocolate Cake Recipe

Recipe Description

Decadent, moist, and full of deep chocolate flavor, this classic chocolate cake is a true showstopper. With a tender crumb and rich cocoa frosting, it's perfect for birthdays, celebrations, or whenever you’re craving something indulgent. Whether served plain or dressed up with berries and whipped cream, it's a timeless treat that never fails to impress.

Ingredients:

    For The Cake

  • 200g (1 ¾ cups) all-purpose flour
  • 75g (⅔ cup) unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 ½ tsp baking powder
  • 1 ½ tsp baking soda
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 200g (1 cup) granulated sugar
  • 100g (½ cup) brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 240ml (1 cup) whole milk
  • 120ml (½ cup) vegetable oil
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 240ml (1 cup) boiling water or hot coffee (for enhanced chocolate flavor)

    • For The Frosting

  • 170g (¾ cup) unsalted butter, softened
  • 300g (2 ½ cups) powdered sugar
  • 75g (⅔ cup) cocoa powder
  • 60ml (¼ cup) milk
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 175°C (350°F): Grease and line two 8-inch (20cm) round cake pans with parchment paper.
  2. Mix dry ingredients: In a large bowl, sift together flour, cocoa powder, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. Add wet ingredients: In another bowl, whisk together both sugars, eggs, milk, oil, and vanilla until smooth. Gradually mix wet into dry ingredients.
  4. Add hot water/coffee: Slowly stir in the boiling water or hot coffee. The batter will be thin - that's normal.
  5. Bake: Divide batter evenly between the two pans. Bake for 30-35 minutes or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.
  6. Cool: Let cakes cool in pans for 10 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely.
  7. Make the frosting: Beat butter until creamy, then sift in powdered sugar and cocoa powder. Add milk, vanilla, and salt; beat until smooth and fluffy.
  8. Assemble: Frost the top of one cake layer, add the second layer, and cover the entire cake with the remaining frosting.
