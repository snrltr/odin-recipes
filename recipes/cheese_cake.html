Odin Cheese Cake Recipe

Recipe Description

Indulge in the rich, creamy decadence of this classic cheesecake recipe, a dessert lover's dream come true. With a buttery graham cracker crust and a smooth, velvety filling made from cream cheese, sugar, and a hint of vanilla, this cheesecake is the perfect balance of sweet and tangy. Baked to perfection and chilled until set, it's a timeless treat that's ideal for any occasion. Whether served plain or topped with fresh berries or a drizzle of chocolate, this cheesecake is sure to impress every time.

Ingredients:

    For The Crust

  • 1 ½ cups (150g) graham cracker or digestive biscuit crumbs
  • ¼ cup (50g) granulated sugar
  • ½ cup (115g) unsalted butter, melted

    • For The Filling

  • 900g (2 lbs) cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup (200g) granulated sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 4 large eggs
  • 2/3 cup (160ml) sour cream
  • 2/3 cup (160ml) heavy cream

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 160°C (325°F): Line the bottom of a 9-inch (23cm) springform pan with parchment paper and lightly grease the sides.
  2. Make the crust: In a bowl, mix graham cracker crumbs, sugar, and melted butter until combined. Press the mixture into the bottom of the prepared pan. Bake for 10 minutes. Set aside to cool slightly.
  3. Prepare the filling: In a large mixing bowl, beat the cream cheese until smooth and creamy (about 2 minutes). Add sugar and vanilla; beat until well combined. Add the eggs one at a time, mixing on low speed after each until just blended. Stir in sour cream and heavy cream until smooth.
  4. Bake: Pour the filling over the crust and smooth the top. Place the pan in a larger baking dish and fill the outer dish with hot water (water bath) to halfway up the sides of the springform pan (optional but helps prevent cracking).Bake for 55-65 minutes, until the edges are set and the center is slightly jiggly.
  5. Cool and chill: Turn off the oven, crack the door, and let the cheesecake cool inside for 1 hour. Remove from oven and chill in the fridge for at least 4 hours or overnight.
  6. Serve: Run a knife around the edge of the cheesecake before releasing the springform pan. Serve plain or top with fruit, chocolate sauce, or whipped cream.
